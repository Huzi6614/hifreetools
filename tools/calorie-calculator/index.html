Formulas used for Calorie Calculator 

The functionality of our best calorie calculator is based on the popular BMR estimation formulas. This tool helps you with the calculation of calories through your desired formula. Here are the equations that run in our backend to provide you with calorie consumption data. 

Mifflin-St Jeor Equation:

(Men) BMR = 10W + 6.25H – 5A + 5

(Women) BMR = 10W + 6.25H – 5A - 161

 Revised Harris-Benedict Equation:

(Men) BMR = 13.397W + 4.799H – 5.677A + 88.362

(Women) BMR = 9.247W + 3.098H – 4.330A + 447.593

Katch-McArdle Equation:

(both men and women) BMR = 370 + 21.6(1 – F) W

For these equations:

W = Weight (in kg)

H = Height (in cm)

A = Age

F = Body Fat Percentage

How to calculate maintenance calories?

Men and women both can calculate maintenance calories by the following formulas:

For Men:

Maintenance Calories = (10 x weight(kg)) + (6.25 x height(cm)) – (5 x age(years)) – 5)

For Women:

Maintenance Calories = (10 x weight(kg)) + (6.25 x height(cm)) – (5 x age(years)) – 161)

For easy calculation, people can take assistance from a maintenance calorie calculator to keep the weight same.

Do your Maintenance Calories Fluctuate?

Maintenance calories refer to the calories that neither cause weight loss nor weight gain - thus maintaining the current body state. These calories are influenced by two primary factors: basal metabolic rate (BMR) and physical activity level (PAL). Change in these factors affects your maintenance calories. Whenever you feel any difference in your body count calories with the help of the maintenance calories calculator.

The empty calories are those calories that come from food items that contain no nutrients. We consume empty calories from a variety of food, such as soft drinks, energy drinks, cakes, cookies, sweets, etc.
