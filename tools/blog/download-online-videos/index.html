Want to download videos from any website for free? Stick around, and I'll show you an easy way to do it. But first of all, did you know that online video content is becoming mainstream? Take a look at these numbers, and you'll understand better:

  • More video content is uploaded online in 30 days than the major U.S. television networks have created in 30 years

  • 87% of online marketers use video content

  • More than 500 million hours of videos are watched on YouTube each day

  • Online videos will account for more than 80% of all consumer internet traffic by 2020.

If anything, these statistics show a pattern: videos are dominating the Internet and gaining so much momentum by the day.
 

 

One reason for this is that video content does what most other content types don't — it combines visual, motion, and audio to grab the viewer's attention and engage them maximally. Another reason is the recent breakthrough in mobile technology and broadband networks. As a result of the enormous mobile penetration, watching videos has become a significant part of users’ online experience. Online marketers love to use videos in their marketing campaigns because of the incredible results. Equally, online content consumers love to watch videos.

 

 

And with more video sharing and streaming websites popping up online, users are turning to videos for educational, informational, and entertainment purposes. With videos, you can learn faster, better, and more accessible.
 

BUT THERE'S A PROBLEM WITH ONLINE VIDEOS

They are expensive to consume.
In short, to watch videos online, you'll need lots of data either from a Wi-Fi network or your mobile network.
And to make things worse? Video hosting sites mostly do not let users download videos. They prefer users to visit their sites repeatedly to watch the videos. Why? Because when users keep coming back again and again, the users would interact more with the locations and thereby generating more revenue and engagement. How? Most of these video sites make money through digital ads; thus, more visitors = more ad clicks = more revenue. See the math there?
