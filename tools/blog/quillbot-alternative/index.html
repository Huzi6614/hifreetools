Hi Free Tools - Best Free Quillbot Alternative

VIEWS: 2550 Views CATEGORY: Education READING TIME: 7 Min To Read UPLOADED ON: 28 Feb 2025

Hi Free Tools Vs QuillBot

Creating high-quality textual content requires more than thorough research. It also requires various checks, such as writing flow, keeping grammar on point, and checking for plagiarism and AI text patterns. Fortunately, technological advancements have made conducting all these and various other checks easier.

Various online tool suites are available that provide all kinds of handy utilities under one roof. Two of the most popular platforms are Hi Free Tools and QuillBot. In this blog post, we'll compare these two digital toolkit resources in great detail.

We'll start by briefly introducing each platform. Then, we'll compare similar tools available on QuillBot and Hi Free Tools. So, let's get the ball rolling without further ado.

Introducing Hi Free Tools VS QuillBot — Brief Overviews

Before comparing QuillBot and Hi Free Tools, it is essential to have a solid understanding and familiarity with both platforms. For this reason, let's start this discussion by sharing a quick rundown of each platform.

Hi Free Tools

Hi Free Tools is a much larger online tools repository than QuillBot. To be more precise, it contains an extensive collection of 300+ utilities that cater to various needs, such as: 

  • Calculation.
  • Conversion.
  • Development.
  • Document management.
  • Graphics designing.
  • Search engine.
  • Text analysis and writing.
  • Web creation and management.

So, it has a much larger audience base than QuillBot. However, like QuillBot, Hi Free Tools has mainly gained popularity due to its text analysis and writing-related utilities, such as plagiarism checker, paraphrasing tool, text summarizer, grammar checker, and AI content detector.

QuillBot

QuillBot is a web-based tool suite platform created mainly as a digital resource for writing assistance. In its early days, it relied on previous technological frameworks. However, since artificial intelligence has revolutionized various aspects of our lives, QuillBot has incorporated different AI algorithms into its utilities.

Comparing the Famous Tools of Hi Free Tools and QuillBot

Hi Free Tools vs QuillBot will only be fair if we test the strengths and weaknesses of similar tools. So, for this blog post, we'll only focus on the writing-related tools of QuillBot and Hi Free Tools. With that in mind, let's dive into the comparison:

  1. Plagiarism Checker

Hi Free Tools

Hi Free Tools, on the other hand, offers a freemium tool. So, you can use this online utility without spending a dime. However, you can only check the originality status of 1000 words for free. This tool supports all the traditional document formats. But the good thing is that you can directly import your files from the following cloud storages:

  • Dropbox.
  • Google Drive.

histream.me/tools also uses different color schemes to find the unoriginal sections in a text piece. However, you must push the action button to start the process here. Upon completing the scan, this utility also generates a plagiarism report. However, its report offers more detailed insights into the stats of a plagiarism scan, such as:

  • Ratio of exact and partially plagiarized sections.
  • Sources' references.
  • Number of words copied from each source.

Additionally, this online tool allows you to exclude specific sources from the scan by simply entering their URLs. Plus, the ability to switch to a deeper detection at the expense of a few bucks is always a handy backup. So, this tool is an excellent mix of basic and advanced similarity detection algorithms. This tool also supports detection in more than 100 languages, which makes it suitable for a global audience.

QuillBot

However, QuillBot is a premium utility that uses real-time analysis to locate a document's uncited sections. That document can be in the following forms:

  • .docx.
  • .html.
  • .pdf.
  • .txt.
  • Raw text.

This online tool uses red and green colors to highlight semi and fully-unoriginal sentences in a text piece. So, its visual richness makes the identification of similarity easier, especially in lengthy documents. Once this web utility completes its analysis, it provides a detailed report that contains the following things:

  • Different types of plagiarism ratios.
  • Links to the sources.

  1. Paraphrasing Tool

Hi Free Tools

Hi Free Tools uses AI to paraphrase the given content. To be more precise, this online utility combines contextual awareness and its training data to paraphrase anything. The tool takes help from Natural Language Processing (NLP) to understand the context, whereas machine learning models help with the actual paraphrasing process. The fusion of NLP and ML empowers this web utility to paraphrase the text and improve its readability, engagement, and vocabulary.

Besides the support of advanced technologies, this web utility uses the following four modes to satisfy diverse needs:

  • Smart Spin.
  • Ultra Spin.
  • Spin Manual.
  • AI Writer.

Although QuillBot's AI paraphraser may seem advantageous in the numbers game, the reality is slightly different. Our tests revealed that all the modes of SmallSEOTool's paraphrasing tool performed on par with the various QuillBot AI paraphraser modes. So, regarding the quality of the actual results, it is a win-win situation.

QuillBot

The main reason for QuillBot's fame online is its paraphraser. It uses advanced AI algorithms and nine modes to paraphrase all types of content. To be more specific, here is a complete list of all the various modes that this online tool uses to revamp the text:

  • Standard.
  • Fluency.
  • Natural.
  • Formal.
  • Academic.
  • Simple.
  • Creative.
  • Expand.
  • Shorten.
  • Custom.

So, whether you want to increase clarity and readability, rewrite new words and vocabulary order, add a personalized (human) touch, change the tone to a formal audience, or alter the length, QuillBot has you covered. Besides the availability of different modes, this utility lets you set the frequency of AI-powered synonym replacement by adjusting the slider to the left or the right.

Additionally, QuillBot supports paraphrasing multilingual content. You can upload your document file from local device storage or paste the copied text to get started. However, if you use the free version, you won't be able to paraphrase more than 125 words in one go.

  1. Text Summarizer

Hi Free Tools

Hi Free Tools is an online freeware tool offering abstractive and extractive summary generation options. Thanks to the integration of AI, more specifically NLP, this tool analyzes the key points from any text piece just like humans. So, no matter how complicated or lengthy your content is, it performs exceptionally well when detecting essential elements.

As far as the actual procedure of summary generation is concerned, Hi Free Tools offers two options:

  • Abstractive.
  • Extractive.

You can use the abstractive approach if you want the results in the bullet points. However, you will see replicated wording and sentence structuring in this mode. For this reason, the best bet is to go with the extractive approach, where this online tool generates an entirely new summary while maintaining the original meaning.

Additionally, this web utility allows you to adjust the summary's length with a slider. However, choosing the mode and setting the length before proceeding with summary generation is essential. Otherwise, this tool won't work as expected.

QuillBot

QuillBot, on the other hand, offers a freemium AI summarizer tool. So, you will face the 600-word count limitation when using this web utility for free. But you can always switch to the premium version to experience uninterrupted and better service.

Like Hi Free Tools, the text summarizer of QuillBot also allows adjusting the output's length with the help of a slider. However, it doesn't use numbers or percentages. Instead, it uses 'Short' and 'Long' words to help users adjust the length of their desired summary. So, it may not be as helpful as SmallSEOTool's summarizing utility.

Regarding the summary generation process, this web utility allows you to select whether you want the output in the 'Paragraph' or 'Bullet Points' form. However, unlike Hi Free Tools, there is no option between abstractive and extractive summarization approaches. So, you may have to carefully compare the input and output to find any duplications. The good thing is when you process a text piece, QuillBot's summarizer tool quickly analyzes the content and provides a list of the most important keywords. So, by selecting your desired keywords, you can refine your summaries better.

Additionally, QuillBot also gives the option to generate a summary with the help of a prompt. So, if you want to use that, you must first enter the text as usual. Once done, you must specify your custom instructions for what kind of summary you want. This feature may give you more control, but is only available for premium members. 

  1. Grammar Checker

Hi Free Tools

Hi Free Tools is a free grammar corrector tool that uses color-coded schemes to locate different writing issues. To be more specific, this online tool uses red to highlight grammar and punctuation mistakes and purple to specify spelling errors. The combination of different colors makes it easier to differentiate between various writing errors, especially in lengthy documents.

Like the aforementioned writing tools of Hi Free Tools, its grammar checker also supports traditional text uploading and direct file importing from cloud storage. To be more precise, you can check a document of 5000 words (max.) as a free user of this web utility.

So, once you've uploaded and processed the text, this tool takes a few moments to locate all the underlying writing errors. Upon completion, click the highlighted sections to explore this utility's contextually-appropriate suggestions. This gives you more control over the entire process, but it will take longer to rectify all the mistakes.

There is also an option for 'Deep Grammar Check,' which is handy for performing advanced proofreading. However, you can only use this option as a premium member. Switching to a premium membership also allows you to check more lengthy documents, which will be a plus point when dealing with research papers.

QuillBot

QuillBot's grammar checker is also a visually rich online tool. It uses different colors to highlight various writing mistakes in the text. However, compared to Hi Free Tools, this web utility functions more as a writing assistant than a grammar checker. So, in addition to identifying various writing mistakes, QuillBot also assigns a writing score to your text based on the following parameters:

  • Grammar.
  • Fluency.
  • Clarity.

There is no direct option to switch between basic and advanced grammar checks here. Getting used to this tool's interface may be challenging if you're a newbie. But this doesn't mean QuillBot's grammar checker is restricted to basic-level proofreading. This tool highlights some advanced writing errors, even if you're a free user. But to rectify the detected advanced issues, you must subscribe to its premium membership.

QuillBot's grammar checker also uses the same contextual understanding as Hi Free Tools. The working mechanism and error detection accuracy of both these tools are similar. The only thing where QuillBot might have a slight advantage is the availability of additional writing stats. But then again, SmallSEOTool's grammar checker has better language support. So, it's a double-win situation.

  1. AI Detector

Hi Free Tools

Hi Free Tools' AI content detector is one of the most visually attractive tools available in the online sector. This web utility uses the same pattern and feature extraction mechanism to identify whether a text piece is AI-based or human-written. Hence, its accuracy is comparable to QuillBot's AI detector.

However, Hi Free Tools' AI content detector is the clear winner regarding the supported text length. For instance, there is no minimum input requirement here. You can check the human content score in a write-up of a few sentences. Similarly, the maximum supported word count limit in Hi Free Tools' AI detector is 2000. Therefore, this web utility also works well with extensive documents.

Hi Free Tools' AI content detector only supports a text piece written in the English language. So, regarding language support, this tool falls behind QuillBot. But you get a visually rich and user-friendly interface here. So, the choice between these two depends totally on your requirements.

QuillBot

QuillBot's AI detector is a freeware online tool that quickly checks whether the entered text is human-written or AI-based. This web utility has been trained on the writing patterns of both AI-generated and human-written text, so it quickly uses the patterns of its training data to differentiate between AI-based and human-written text.

Due to the diversity of QuillBot AI detector's training data, this online tool can also identify whether you've refined your AI-generated and human-written text. So, it offers comprehensive AI text detection results. But this web utility only works for a text piece of 80-1200 words. This means it can't verify a content piece for AI detection if its word count is lower than 80 words and higher than 1200 words. Hence, this online tool is unsuitable for documents that are both too small and too large.

This web utility can check for AI-generated and human-written patterns in Spanish, French, German, and English content, so it supports most major languages.

Summing Up — Concluding Remarks

In conclusion, accuracy and originality are key to a perfectly written content piece. So, to maintain both these, Hi Free Tools and QuillBot are two of the best options available in the online market space. Both tool suites offer cutting-edge plagiarism checking, AI text detection, grammar checking and correction, AI-powered text paraphrasing, and AI-based summary generation services. However, Hi Free Tools has a slight edge regarding cost-effectiveness and feature availability. QuillBot also shines in some areas. However, the output quality of most tools is on par with that of their counterparts. For these reasons, there is no ultimate winner here because the criteria for being the best depends on an individual's needs and preferences.
