Easily and Quickly Convert PDF Files to Word Documents

VIEWS: 18573 Views CATEGORY: Technology READING TIME: 9 Min To Read UPLOADED ON: 20 Nov 2018

If you've been using the Internet for a reasonable amount of time now, chances are that you have, at least once, interacted with a document — whether to create, store, view, or share it.

And you're not alone!

In this digital age, documents play a massive role in how people and organisations communicate with each other, as well as how they store and share information.

The undeniable fact is this:

Every document must be in a given format. Otherwise, you wouldn't be able to store, view, or do anything with it. There currently exist several document formats — .PDF, .DOC, .RTF, .HTML and so many more — and each one has its limitations and high points. As a result, it is often necessary to change a document from one format to another to be able to use it as freely as you want.

 

But particularly in this guide, we want to show you how to easily and quickly convert PDF documents to Word files (DOC and DOCX).

 

WHAT ARE PDF AND WORD FILES?

 

PDF and Word (.DOC and . DOCX) are universally accepted file formats in which any type of document (business, personal, etc.) can be saved. But there are differences between the two (more on that later).

 PDF stands for Portable Document Format. It is a file format used in viewing and reliably sharing documents, independent of hardware, software, or operating system.

 

Initially created by Adobe, PDF is now an open standard maintained by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO). PDF files can be created using Adobe Acrobat, Acrobat Capture, or similar tools. On the other hand, DOC and DOCX are file formats used by Microsoft Word (although they are also compatible with most word processing software, including OpenOffice and Apple Pages).

 

A DOC/DOCX file can contain formatted text, images, graphs, tables, charts, page formatting, and print settings.

The main difference between DOC and DOCX is their current status.

 

The DOC format has been the default file format for Microsoft Word until the 2003 version (i.e., Microsoft Word 97, 2000, XP, and 2003). Microsoft introduced DOCX as the new default format for Word in 2007, 2010, 2013, and 2016.


 

THE DIFFERENCE BETWEEN PDF AND WORD FILES

 

The most significant difference between PDF and Word formats is their content delivery and editing abilities. While Word files can easily be edited using Microsoft Word (or any other compatible word processor), PDFs aren't designed primarily for content editing. Instead, PDFs are built for content delivery.

PDFs can retain all the layouts, structures, and formatting contained in a document and is viewable by all platforms. This means PDF can create a WYSIWYG (What You See Is What You Get) file which assures you that what you see on your screen is exactly what other people will see on their screens or even in print.

 

However, PDF's editing capability is limited.

 

Word (DOC and DOCX) formats are easily editable. When a user wants to edit a file, they must go back to the word processor and get it done. But it isn't a good idea to share critical documents in Word formats as they may likely not maintain the original layout and structure.


 

WHY CONVERT PDF TO DOC?

 

In a nutshell, the main reason you want to convert your PDF file to a Word document is to be able to edit it, plus do all the things you can only do with DOC files. In other words, given that PDFs were created as a delivery format and not for editing, it becomes necessary to convert PDFs to Word so that it is easily editable. PDF is an open-source program, thus giving any individual or developer the right to develop editing/conversion tools for it as opposed to Microsoft’s proprietary software.

By converting, you'll be turning all your great work stored in PDF into a Word document that’s easy to update.
 

HOW TO CONVERT PDF FILES TO WORD — MANUAL VS AUTOMATED PROCESSES

 

In converting a PDF to Word, you can do it manually or use an automated tool.

Manual conversion, as you'd expect, is highly laborious and time-consuming. It involves opening a new Word file, retyping the words one by one as they appear on the PDF (or copying and pasting the same), trying to import the images and graphics (pretty tricky), and much more.

 

This manual-based process is prone to human errors and can lead to formatting conflicts as the text structure won't stay the same as in the original format of the PDF. On the other hand, there's the automated process, which is quite the opposite of the manual one. Thus, most people often naturally choose to use this method.

PDF VS DOC: WHICH IS BETTER?


It depends on what you want to do. Each has strengths and weaknesses. To help you make the right decision, here's a quick run down of when it’s appropriate to use each format:

When to use Word Format? 


Word Processing: Unlike PDFs, Word files can easily be updated using Microsoft Word or any other compatible word processor. Plus, word processors provide the added benefits of spell-checking and grammar-checking, among many other helpful writing tools. Editing: As discussed above, Word is built primarily for document editing. It contains intuitive formatting and compositional devices that allow you to edit and format your documents efficiently. Reusing Images: Images in PDFs are embedded. Thus they can't be extracted and reused. But Word is the opposite. You can yank images from it and reuse them in other documents.
 

When to Use PDF Format?


Retaining File Format: PDF can maintain your file formats, making it great for archiving and transfer. Business and legal documents are examples of documents that must have their exact appearance for security and integrity. Online Content: If you're looking to make your documents (ebooks, newsletters, technical manuals, etc.) available online, then you'd want to use PDF. With PDF, you can secure your document so that it can only be read and not altered.

Printing: With its WYSIWYG capability, PDF can send print-ready files to any printer. So if your goal is to produce a high-quality print job in which precise page layout and high-resolution images are vital, then you'd want to use PDF.

 

CONCLUSION


Both PDF and Word are popular document formats. But each has its limitations and advantages.

The Word format is the best option for editing and making changes to works-in-progress documents, while the PDF format is the preferred choice for viewing and transferring documents. We recommend adopting a strategy that incorporates both of these formats.
