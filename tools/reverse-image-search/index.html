Reverse Photo Lookup - How It Works?

In simple words, a reverse photo lookup is an advanced online search method in which you upload an image (instead of entering a text-based or voice-based keyword) to find information based on the query.

With Google image search, you can quickly find similar images from around the web and obtain relative information about a photo, including objects or places contained in it.

Content-Based Visual Information Retrieval (CBVIR)

Reverse picture search works by using a query technique called content-based image retrieval (CBIR) — also known as query by image content (QBIC) and content-based visual information retrieval (CBVIR). This advanced image search technology works by identifying the elements contained in an image. From objects to people, color, and location, everything is considered against your photo search query to retrieve similar pictures.
