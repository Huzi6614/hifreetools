Subtract or Add Time Manually

The process of time subtraction and addition manually can be a hectic task. If you have the entire data in hours, minutes, or seconds, and you wish to get its representation in days, then you’ll need to make several conversions. To calculate time manually, you need to remember that:

  • 1 day = 24 hours = 1440 hours = 86400 seconds

After remembering these, you can manually add or subtract time to calculate the time gap.

 Add Time Calculator

Tired of struggling to add up the total time you worked? Our add time calculator makes it easy! Just input values for days, hours, minutes, or seconds to find accurate answers. Here are a few examples that can help you understand the entire time addition processing.

Example:

Suppose that in the 1st week of January, you worked for 72 hours. In the second week, you worked 84 hours.

What is the total time that you worked in the first two weeks of January?

Solution:

As the values are in hours, you can easily add them:

= 84 + 72

= 156

You worked 156 hours in the first two weeks of January. However, the role of the adding time calculator isn’t done here. As a single day has 24 hours, this calculator will turn your time into days by:

= 156/24

= 6.5

6.5 means that you have worked for 6 and a half days in the span of two weeks. This half-day will be converted to hours:

= 0.5 x 24

= 12

So, finally, the actual time you’ve worked for in January’s two weeks is 6 days and 12 hours.

Subtract Time Calculator

Subtracting two times from each other is also challenging like adding them but with the availability of a time subtractor, it's quite easy. Let’s see some of its examples below:

Example:

In February, Alex worked 372 hours. In March, he worked for 444 hours. How many more days Alex worked in March as compared to February?

Solution:

As the information is available in hours, they need to be converted in days in order to find the difference.

If we simply find the time gap between Alex’s working hours in February and March, it can be deduced that:

= 444 – 372

= 72

So, Alex worked 72 hours more in March as compared to February. In order to get its details in days, you need to:

72/24

= 3

Hence, from this time subtraction calculator, it can be concluded that Alex worked 3 days more in March.
